Computing for the Social Good

Bringing Awarness to Social Issues
Through Computer Science Education

Our Research

Focused on bringing enrichment to computer science course curricula, Computing for the Social Good is a research area that challenges students to think critically about how their learning relates to pressing social issues. It reinforces the idea that computer scientists at their core are problem-solvers—what better way to build upon this notion than by having students reflect on these problems and propose plausible solutions?

Currently, the research area is targeted towards introductory level computer science courses, with emphasis on connecting every project to some real-world social crisis. The contents of this website showcase just this—with links to download programming projects that were developed to be both informative and engaging. Additionally, our collection of data sets features a wide range of fields that are worth exploring. Lastly, to delve deeper into the research area, consider browsing the bibliography page for in-dept analysis from experienced teaching faculty.

Featured Projects
Solar Batteries

Recent surges in the demand for electricity have prompted states to implement new ways of generating power. One such measure is the use of solar batteries—clean energy sources that utilize sunlight for dispensing electricity.

This project is ideal for introducing lists or 1D arrays. The objective is for students to use these data structures to determine if the supply from a solar battery is sufficient in meeting the demand for electricity based on the criteria provided.

Butterfly Migration

During their yearly migration, monarch butterflies follow a trail of milkweed plants positioned in various regions of North America. These plants not only serve as food and shelter for these insects, but also provide scientists with key insights into migration patterns.

This project requires the use of 2D arrays. It prompts students to design a program that acts as a compass for humans to follow based on the location of milkweed plants situated across the grid.

Cholesterol Tracking

Heart disease is among the leading causes of death in the United States. To prevent this illness, medical experts recommend monitoring your dietary intake and making decisions that promote a healthy lifestyle.

This project focuses solely on scalars. It challenges students to design a program that serves as health tracker to indicate whether or not a person is at risk for heart disease.

Datasets

Energy Consumption

This expansive data set from the US Energy Information Administration contains data on several energy production methods, the consumption by commercial industries, and the cost associated with the entire process.

Heart Disease

This Kaggle.com publication presents data on risk factors related to heart disease. One of our projects uses this data to develop a model that informs the user if they are at risk for contracting this illness.

Food Access

Acquired from the United States Department of Agriculture, this data set contains information about the US population and varying distances from residential areas to the closest grocery store.
