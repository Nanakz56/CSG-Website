Programming Projects

A Library of Programming Projects Suited to the Needs of Introductory-Level Computer Science Courses

Scalar-Based Problems

Housing Crisis

Densely populated areas (New York City, for instance) typically have high mortgage/rental costs. As the demand for these living spaces increases, so do the associated costs. For low-income households, this presents a challenge as their income is not sufficient to meet the skyrocketing prices. It is only a matter of time then before they will need to relocate to other accommodations.

This project highlights the need for affordable housing costs. Given a selection of variables, it requires that students design a program that determines if a resident will be able to afford living in such an area after some amount of time.

Cholesterol Tracking

Heart disease is among the leading causes of death in the United States. To prevent this illness, medical experts recommend monitoring your dietary intake and making decisions that promote a healthy lifestyle.

This project challenges students to design a program that serves as health tracker to indicate whether or not a person is at risk for heart disease.

Waste Management

Overflowing landfills. Illegal dumping in Southeast Asia. Environmental Pollution. What do these three issues have in common? They are all linked to careless human behavior.

This project challenges students to examine the impact of excessive waste production on land usability and how it diminishes our finite natural resources.

Utilizing Data Structures

Solar Batteries

Recent surges in the demand for electricity have prompted states to implement new ways of generating power. One such measure is the use of solar batteries—clean energy sources that utilize sunlight for dispensing electricity.

This project is ideal for introducing lists or 1D arrays. The objective is for students to use these data structures to determine if the supply from a solar battery is sufficient in meeting the demand for electricity based on the criteria provided.

Let's Talk About Aquifers

Farmers have always relied on aquifers to irrigate their crops. In recent years, however, overuse of these underground water supplies has led to severe depletion. Since more water is being pumped than replenished, it is only a matter of years until this resource is completely drained.

Proper regulation when it comes to managing natural resources is key to ensuring that they last for future generations—this is is the main idea that this project hopes to covey to students as they are designing their program.

Unwanted Neighbors

A psychological study suggests that people tend to gravitate towards others who share similar interests. To build on this idea, consider a neighborhood made up of dog and cat owners. Over time, dog owners would prefer to live near their fellow dog owners, and the same could be true for cat owners as well.

This project makes use of 2D arrays. Students are to simulate a neighborhood change over the course of a year utilizing a two-dimensional grid and the “rules” to follow for relocating residents.

Butterfly Migration

During their yearly migration, monarch butterflies follow a trail of milkweed plants positioned in various regions across North America. These plants not only serve as food and shelter for these insects, but also provide scientists with key insights into migration patterns.

This project requires the use of 2D arrays. It prompts students to design a program that acts as a compass for humans to follow based on the location of milkweed plants situated across the grid.

The Pantry Run

A trip to the grocery store can become a hassle if lengthy distances and inadequate means of transportation are considered. For residents living in areas with limited access to a grocery store/pantry, this could present a problem that could impact their diet.

This project tests students' ability to process scalars and basic data structures. They are to write a program that considers a set of variables pertaining to a grocery/pantry trip. The program is to print out calculations pertaining to distance, accessibility, and mode of transportation needed.

Data Integration

Heart Rate Sustainability

Using data acquired from Kaggle.com, this project requires students to design a program that outputs a health score. This score is calculated using various factors such as age, weight, cholesterol level, etc. The idea is for the program to act as health tracker, informing the user about how healthy they are based on the inputs given.

The higher the score, the closer the person is to developing heart disease or other forms of illness. To note, as this is an introductory-level project, it should not be used as an alternative to seeking expert medical advice or replacement for technologies that have been proven to work.

Food Insecurity

To live in a food desert means to live in an area where food sources are not easily accessible. In rural areas, for instance, where residents live more than 10 miles from the nearest grocery store, this presents a challenge for meeting their nutritional needs. This is especially so for when residents have a limited access to transportation and/or are of a low-income status.

This project makes use of data collected by the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA). It challenges students to perform data computations to generate insights into areas deemed to be food deserts, and to grasp the need for improved resource access locations amongst certain groups of people.
